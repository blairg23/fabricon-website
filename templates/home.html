Fabricon was founded in 1975 by Certified Master Fabric Craftsman Mark Welander manufacturing era authentic Native American tipis that are still on display at the Smithsonian Museum in Washington DC, The Cody Museum in Cody, Wyoming and the Museum of Man in Toronto Ontario Canada. As well as designing and manufacturing high quality horse packing gear and wall tents that were later featured in the Oscar award winning film Dances With Wolves.

In 1979 Fabricon expanded to commercial and residential awning manufacturing that can be seen all over Western Montana including the Caras Park Pavilion in downtown Missoula, MT. Todays evolution of the fabric industry has enabled Fabricon to be apart of several national and international projects that have been honored with International Achievement Awards from the IFIA (Industrial Fabrics Association International).
